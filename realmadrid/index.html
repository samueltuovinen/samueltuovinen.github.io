Real Madrid CF

History

Real Madrid is a Spanish professional footbal club based in Madrid. Real Madrid is founded in 1902 as Madrid Football Club, the club has traditionally worn a white home kit since inception. The honorific title Real is Spanish for “Royal” and was bestowed to the club by King Alfonso XIII in 1920 together with the royal crown in the emblem. The club has won 34 La Liga titles, 19 Copa del Rey, and 13 UEFA Champions League titles.

Madrid

Madrid is the capital and most populous city of Spain. The city has almost 3.4 million inhabitants and a metropolitan area population of approximately 6.7 million. It is the second-largest city in the European Union.

Santiago Bernabeu

The team has played its home matches in the 81,044-capacity Santiago Bernabeu Stadium in downtown Madrid since 1947. It is the second-largest stadium in Spain. It is named after footballer and former Real Madrid president Santiago Bernabeu. A €525-million renovation project started in 2019 and the new Bernabeu is set to be ready in 2022. The capacity will be increased by approximately 4000 and a retractable roof as well as a retractable pitch will be added.
